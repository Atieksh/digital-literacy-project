# Phishing Case Study

## Title: Email Phishing Attack Example

## Introduction

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and banking details by pretending to be a trusted source. It is one of the most common cyber threats faced by internet users.

## Case Description

In this case study, a phishing email was sent to users pretending to be from a bank. The email informed users that their bank account would be blocked if they did not update their account details immediately.

The email included a fake link that looked like the official bank website. When users clicked the link, they were redirected to a fake webpage designed to collect login credentials.

Many users who entered their information had their banking details stolen, leading to financial losses.

## Impact of the Attack

- Loss of sensitive personal information  
- Financial fraud and monetary loss  
- Unauthorized access to user accounts  
- Loss of trust in digital systems  

## Conclusion

This case study highlights the importance of identifying phishing emails and avoiding suspicious links. Awareness and proper cybersecurity practices can help prevent such attacks.
